No network needed. Mines a synthetic job so you can see — and verify — real SHA‑256d work and your phone's hashrate.

Connects to a real Stratum pool through a WebSocket bridge (see README). Solo/lottery pools pay the whole block reward to the finder.

Sends mining.suggest_difficulty. The pool may honor it or clamp to its own floor — watch the log for the “set share difficulty” it replies with. This changes how often you see shares, never your odds of a block. Some pools instead read difficulty from the password field (e.g. d=1000).

Reality check. A phone does ~106 hashes/sec; the Bitcoin network does ~1020. Your chance of finding a block is comparable to the proverbial monkey typing Shakespeare. That's the point — it's a conversation piece. Don't mine for money; mine to understand.

Open-source. No wallet access, no telemetry. Your address is only sent to the pool you choose.
